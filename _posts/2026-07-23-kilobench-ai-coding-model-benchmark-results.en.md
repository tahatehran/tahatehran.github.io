KiloBench is an AI coding model benchmark that ranks the most capable coding models by cost versus performance. If you want to know which model gives the best coding results for the lowest price, KiloBench publishes official scores you can sort by capability, cost, or token usage.

## What KiloBench Measures

KiloBench evaluates models on Terminal Bench 2.0, a benchmark built for real-world coding tasks. The results are averaged per complete benchmark attempt, so you see actual task success rates rather than narrow test scores.

## Why It Matters

Model choice in coding tools has become a budget decision. Frontier models like GPT and Claude are powerful, but their per-token costs add up quickly across large codebases. Free or low-cost models can save money, but they sometimes fail on complex reasoning, long context, or multi-file refactoring.

KiloBench gives you numbers instead of marketing claims. You can compare models head-to-head and pick the one that fits your workflow and budget.

## Key Findings

- **Frontier models still lead on hard reasoning**: The top-ranked models are usually frontier paid models. They maintain an edge on architecture, debugging, and large refactoring tasks.
- **Cost efficiency has improved**: Several mid-tier models now deliver frontier-like results at a fraction of the token cost. KiloBench highlights which ones are reliable for daily coding.
- **Context length matters**: Models with 128K or 200K context windows outperform smaller-context models on large-file analysis and repository-wide changes.
- **Free models are catching up**: Some free models now compete with paid models on specific task types, especially if your workflow is constrained to single-file edits, explanations, or documentation.

## How to Use KiloBench

1. Visit the KiloBench leaderboard and sort by the metric that matters to your team.
2. Note the top 2-3 models for your task type.
3. Test them in your own environment with real pull requests, bug fixes, or refactors.
4. Measure your actual completion rate, review burden, and token spend.
5. Pick the model that balances quality and cost for your specific workflow.

## Limitations

Benchmarks are not everything. A model that scores well on KiloBench might still underperform on your codebase if your stack, language, or review standards differ from the benchmark design. Always validate with your own projects before locking in a model for production use.
