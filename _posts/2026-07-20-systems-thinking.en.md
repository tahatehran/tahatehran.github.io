Modern software problems are rarely about a single service or a single language. They are about interaction, failure modes, and long-term evolution.

## 🧩 What Is Systems Thinking

It is the practice of understanding how components influence each other instead of optimizing parts in isolation.

## 🔥 Common Pitfalls

- Optimizing for a single metric while degrading overall reliability
- Copying patterns from unrelated architectures
- Ignoring operational cost until it becomes crisis-driven

## ✅ Useful Practices

- Draw real data-flow diagrams, not just box diagrams
- Review incidents at the system level, not only at the code level
- Prefer reversible decisions in architecture when uncertainty is high

## 📌 Takeaway

Good systems thinking does not prevent complexity, but it makes complexity manageable.
