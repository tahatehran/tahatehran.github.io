Choosing the right database is one of the most critical decisions in software architecture. In this article, we explore the fundamental differences between SQL and NoSQL.

## 📊 SQL Databases (Relational)
These databases (like MySQL and PostgreSQL) use a tabular structure and fixed schema. They are excellent for structured data and financial transactions.

## 📂 NoSQL Databases (Non-Relational)
Databases like MongoDB are designed for unstructured data and horizontal scalability. They offer high flexibility for rapid data model changes.

The choice between the two depends on your project requirements and data types.
